Learning Image Editing Skills.

GIMP stands for GNU Image Manipulation Program. It is a free imaging editing software that works kind of like Photoshop, but better. I used it to learn how to retouch photos, play with layers, and get creative with before and after edits. It’s a great tool for improving pictures or making fun graphics without needing any fancy software.

This project was all about learning how to edit images using GIMP. I focused on building practical skills such as cropping, adjusting color and contrast, removing backgrounds, layering text, and retouching images. One of my favorite parts was taking an ordinary photo and making it look more polished and professional just with a few simple tools. I’ve included before and after examples of my edits to show what I learned. This experience taught me how important visuals can be in presentations, websites, and even health-related content. It also gave me a new appreciation for design work and how editing tools can be used to communicate ideas clearly and creatively.
