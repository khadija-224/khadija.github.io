Using Dartfish for Performance Analysis in Sports

For this project, I used Dartfish, a video analysis tool often used in sports and kinesiology to break down movement. Although Dartfish is a paid software, we were able to access it during class and explore its features.

The above Youtube video analyzes key movement patterns and technique of a track and field sprint. In the video, Dartfish tools like angles, lines, slow motion, and text were used to highlight key technical points. The video pauses at specific frames to highlight different feedback. For example, the third frame was paused to give feedback about “not bending the upper back” and “applying pressure with the entire foot.” These annotations help visualize proper technique and give insight into how small changes can improve performance. Beyond improving performance, this type of analysis is also valuable for injury prevention and rehabilitation. It allows professionals to spot inefficient or risky movement patterns and correct them early on.

In addition to using the desktop version, I also used the mobile app to practice applying similar techniques. However, the mobile app is a simplified version of the desktop. I really enjoyed the hands-on experience of video analysis.
